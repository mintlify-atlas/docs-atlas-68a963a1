## Overview

The **PERMA+H** framework extends Martin Seligman's original PERMA model (2011) by adding Health as a sixth dimension. Together, these six domains provide comprehensive coverage of human flourishing:

| Dimension | Full Name | Description |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **P** | Positive Emotion | Hedonic wellbeing - joy, contentment, pleasure |
| **E** | Engagement | Flow states, autonomy, immersion in meaningful activity |
| **R** | Relationships | Social support, connection, positive relations |
| **M** | Meaning | Purpose in life, life satisfaction, coherence |
| **A** | Accomplishment | Achievement, mastery, material success |
| **H** | Health | Physical and mental health, healthy life expectancy |

<Info>
These six dimensions are **not independent** - they form a tightly coupled system where improvements in one domain tend to support growth in others. This interdependence is the foundation of the [f-factor](/concepts/f-factor).
</Info>

## Detailed Dimension Breakdown

### P: Positive Emotion

**Definition**: The hedonic component of wellbeing - subjective feelings of happiness, joy, contentment, and pleasure.

**Key characteristics**:
- Most direct measure of "feeling good"
- Shortest time horizon (momentary to daily)
- Correlates ~0.55 with overall wellbeing
- Anti-correlates ~-0.45 with negative affect (p-factor)

**Data mappings**:
- **WHR**: Positive affect (Gallup World Poll item)
- **MIDUS 2**: `B1SPOSPA` - Positive Affect composite (1-5 scale, mean of ~7 items)
- **OECD BLI**: Implicit in Life Satisfaction scores

<Warning>
Positive affect data is often _missing_ from country-level datasets. WHR 2024 GitHub mirrors may not include this column. Scripts gracefully skip if unavailable.
</Warning>

```python
# From 01_download_wellbeing_data.py - PERMA mapping
WHR_COLUMN_MAP = {
    "positive affect": "P_positive_emotion",
    # ... other mappings
}
```

### E: Engagement

**Definition**: Flow states, autonomy, self-direction, and immersion in meaningful activity. The "absorption" component of wellbeing.

**Key characteristics**:
- Difficult to measure (people in flow can't self-report during the state)
- Proxy: autonomy and freedom to make life choices
- Eudaimonic dimension ("being your best self")
- Correlates with personal growth and mastery

**Data mappings**:
- **WHR**: "Freedom to make life choices" (autonomy proxy)
- **MIDUS 2**: Two Ryff subscales serve as E proxies:
  - `B1SPWBA1` - Autonomy (self-direction)
  - `B1SPWBG1` - Personal Growth (development/engagement)
- **OECD BLI**: Work-Life Balance (partial proxy)

<Note>
**Why two MIDUS subscales?** Ryff's 6-dimensional model was developed independently of PERMA. Autonomy and Personal Growth both capture aspects of engagement/self-actualization.
</Note>

### R: Relationships

**Definition**: Quality of social connections, social support, positive relations with others.

**Key characteristics**:
- Strongest predictor of long-term wellbeing
- Protective factor against mental and physical illness
- Most consistent across cultures (universal human need)
- Correlates 0.34-0.59 with other Ryff subscales

**Data mappings**:
- **WHR**: "Social support" (do you have someone to count on?)
- **MIDUS 2**: `B1SPWBR1` - Positive Relations with Others
- **OECD BLI**: Community indicator (social network support)

### M: Meaning

**Definition**: Sense of purpose, direction, and significance in life. Belief that one's life has coherence and value.

**Key characteristics**:
- Eudaimonic dimension ("life well-lived")
- Most cognitive/evaluative component
- Correlates 0.60-0.66 with mastery and self-acceptance
- Often measured via life satisfaction (Cantril ladder)

**Data mappings**:
- **WHR**: Cantril ladder / "Ladder score" (0-10 life evaluation)
- **MIDUS 2**: Two indicators:
  - `B1SPWBU1` - Purpose in Life (Ryff primary measure)
  - `B1SB1` - Overall Life Satisfaction (0-10 scale, supplemental)
- **OECD BLI**: Life Satisfaction indicator

<Info>
The Cantril ladder asks: "Imagine a ladder with steps numbered 0 to 10, where 0 is the worst possible life and 10 is the best. Where do you stand today?" This is a _cognitive evaluation_, not momentary affect.
</Info>

### A: Accomplishment

**Definition**: Achievement, mastery, material success, and sense of environmental control.

**Key characteristics**:
- Most culturally variable dimension
- Includes both objective (income, education) and subjective (sense of mastery) components
- Correlates 0.56-0.77 with other eudaimonic dimensions
- Strongest correlation: A ↔ Self-Acceptance (0.77)

**Data mappings**:
- **WHR**: Log GDP per capita (material achievement proxy)
- **MIDUS 2**: `B1SPWBE1` - Environmental Mastery (sense of control/competence)
- **OECD BLI**: Jobs & Earnings, Income & Wealth, Education & Skills
- **World Bank**: School enrollment, employment indicators

### H: Health

**Definition**: Physical and mental health status. Added to PERMA because health both enables and results from flourishing.

**Why add Health to the original PERMA?**

1. **Bidirectional causality**: Good health enables engagement and positive emotion; flourishing promotes health behaviors
2. **Empirical evidence**: Health loads strongly on the f-factor (typically λ ~ 0.35-0.45)
3. **Policy relevance**: Health is a concrete, measurable outcome that policymakers care about
4. **Capability requirement**: Nussbaum's capability approach identifies health as a fundamental threshold

**Data mappings**:
- **WHR**: Healthy Life Expectancy at birth (years)
- **MIDUS 2**: `B1SA11W` - Self-Rated Health (1=excellent → 5=poor, _reversed_ to 1-5 good)
- **OECD BLI**: Health Status indicator
- **World Bank**: Life expectancy, Health expenditure % GDP

<Note>
**Health scaling**: WHR uses population-level life expectancy (40-80 year range), while MIDUS uses individual self-ratings (1-5 Likert). Always check scale and direction when mapping!
</Note>

## Interdependence: The Mutualism Hypothesis

PERMA+H dimensions are **not independent**. Empirical correlations:

| Pair | Typical r | Interpretation |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| M ↔ A | 0.65-0.77 | Purpose drives achievement; success reinforces meaning |
| R ↔ M | 0.52-0.59 | Relationships provide purpose; purpose motivates connection |
| E ↔ M | 0.60 | Engagement in meaningful activity is intrinsically rewarding |
| P ↔ all | 0.40-0.55 | Positive emotion is both cause and consequence of flourishing |
| H ↔ all | 0.35-0.45 | Health enables all domains; all domains protect health |

From `04_individual_level_f_analysis.py:200-208` - known MIDUS 2 benchmarks:

```python
MIDUS_CORR_MATRIX = np.array([
    # Auton   Mastery  Growth   PoRel   Purpose  Self-Acc
    [1.00,    0.47,    0.47,    0.34,    0.44,    0.40],  # Autonomy (E)
    [0.47,    1.00,    0.56,    0.54,    0.65,    0.77],  # Mastery (A)
    [0.47,    0.56,    1.00,    0.47,    0.60,    0.49],  # Growth (E)
    [0.34,    0.54,    0.47,    1.00,    0.52,    0.59],  # Pos Relations (R)
    [0.44,    0.65,    0.60,    0.52,    1.00,    0.66],  # Purpose (M)
    [0.40,    0.77,    0.49,    0.59,    0.66,    1.00],  # Self-Acceptance (P)
])
```

**Weakest pair**: Autonomy ↔ Positive Relations (0.34) - "most distinct subscale"  
**Strongest pair**: Mastery ↔ Self-Acceptance (0.77) - nearly redundant after removing measurement error

This interdependence is formalized in [Model 2: Mutualism](/concepts/mathematical-models#model-2-mutualism), where PERMA+H dimensions support each other through coupled growth dynamics.

## Implementation Notes

<Warning>
**Variable naming conventions**: Column names differ across data sources. The scripts use a standardized `{LETTER}_description` format:
- WHR: `M_meaning_life_sat` (snake_case, descriptive)
- MIDUS: `B1SPWBU1` (original ICPSR codes preserved)
- Always check the mapping dictionaries in script headers!
</Warning>

**Missing data handling**:
- WHR: ~20% missing on Positive Affect (not in all mirrors)
- MIDUS: ~10% missing on auxiliary variables (affects, health)
- Scripts drop rows with _any_ missing value on core PERMA dimensions for complete-case analysis
- Pairwise deletion available for supplemental analyses
